Problem_B Description:

We have a wooden plank of the length n units. Some ants are walking on the plank, each ant moves with speed 1 unit per second. Some of the ants move to the left, the other move to the right.
When two ants moving in two different directions meet at some point, they change their directions and continue moving again. Assume changing directions doesn't take any additional time.
When an ant reaches one end of the plank at a time t, it falls out of the plank imediately.
Given an integer n and two integer arrays left and right, the positions of the ants moving to the left and the right. Return the moment when the last ant(s) fall out of the plank.

category: Data Structure(Array) -> fire pokemon!

Intuition:

While I should admit that this problem looks inintimidating at the first glance, we need to find the pattern to solve this probelm. It is easy to solve the time for a specifi ant to reach the end, however, the difficult part comes in when the ant changes directions.

In order to solve this problem, we need to observe that even if two ants change their directions after meeting at one point, the maximum time for both of them to reach the end actually does not change.

Let's say that left_ant takes left_time to reach the right end, and right_ant takes right_time to reach left end. If they change the direction, left_ant becomes right ant, and it still needs right_time to reach left end. Similar thing happens at the left_ant. Therefore the maximum time for all ants to reach the end, is just the max(time for all left_moving ants reach left end, time for all right_moving ants reach right_end). More specifically, the time for all left_moving ants reach the right is just the last element for left array, similar thing for the right array.

Problem_B Solution: O(nlogn)
